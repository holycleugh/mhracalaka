From the lesser thaumatious bibliotheca,
The heartstrings of the Erameneia unwind,
The fifth of seven threads by Almete sewn.

The actrix Genymente, called Aezeides,
        called Makragnathe, called Agracheron, called Keraunocheron;

Her lords Rame, called Erebe or Eramene,
        and Hete, called Aulaxis or Anechystere;
        and their elder lords Leukomene and Pherousa;

And the auspicious devotees of megazena Koussa,
        the auspicious sisters loud-roaring Kleone and cloud-gathering Zenikousa,
        the auspicious daughters of Diakleia, who hears everything.

“Thus did Genymente great-jawed pray to her mire for glory,
Prayed to the purifier, saying ‘Aezys! my mother who resteth
’Twixt the embrace of celestial Zena and Gaia she chthonic:
Lift up my spirit! my body! that I may find my lord Hete.’

Shining like emeralds, the thoughtful Pitheke gleamed now enormous,
Beamed like the sea called Aegea just before Helies voyage
Crossing the Borean channels flooded by Algeas weeping,
Cruxed by remorse when her daughter fell from those heavenly dactyls;
So leapt unfathomable Bele from that unfathomable barrow,
Leapt across Hyperionian oceans from Zenas divine mount.”

Erameneia 5:1–10.
