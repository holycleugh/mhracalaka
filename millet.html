A sack of millet, and a very berry meditation.


A sack of millet.

From the falling leaves of the candlenut.

Of the heavenly bodies,
The earth is the heavenly body on which we live;
The sun is the greatest heavenly body seen from the earth;
The moon is the next-greatest heavenly body seen from the earth;
The cetsin are sun and the moon together.

Of the cleft of the earth,
The north are the lands from which the moon is seen;
The south are the lands from which nothing is seen;
The withnorth are the lands from which the cetsin are seen;
The withsouth are the lands from which the sun is seen;
The inner earth spans from the north to the withnorth;
The outer earth spans from the withsouth to the south.

Of the spinning of heavenly bodies,
The spinaxle is the line in the worldroom about which spins some body;
The moonly spinaxle or moonaxle is the spinaxle of the moon;
The earthly spinaxle or earthaxle is the spinaxle of the earth.

Of the fellowship between the earth and the moon,
The lease northmark is the northern spot hit by the moonaxle;
The true northmark is the northern spot hit by the earthaxle;
The true southmark is the southern spot hit by the earthaxle;
The warp or thwartrood is the southern spot which begets a flip in handedness.

The earthplot is a rightnook, such that

  • Its middlemark is matched with the true northmark;
  • Its nooks are matched with each other and with the true southmark;
  • Its lain sides when stuck together, are the outer midring;
  • Its stood sides when stuck together, go between withnorth and south;
  • To go into the true southmark, is to come out from the wither true southmark.

The earthplot sameshifts as the warped hoop.

I am the earthplot. I stick together my lain sides; now I am the pipe, my stood sides are rings, and my four true southmarks become two. Then, I stick together my stood sides; now I am the hoop, and my two true southmarks become one. Then, at my one true southmark is my warp;

If from the inner midring the moon is always seen, then the moon is in the belly of the warped hoop in the worldroom.

I draw a lain line through my middlemark. Then, I sameshift as the warped hood; now the line is my inner midring. Then, in my belly is the moon;



A very berry meditation.

Lá wé wéel kirta, el nhénhéyu nhemba;
Je ta wéel nhéa, el ta nhemba nhéyu.
Kur bur śun yar– ber– quf–
Kur gá núge, el tagge nhonge.

Kir kirtel ri kir, kir yargel ri yar, kir berśel ri ber, kir quflel ri quf;
Yar yargel ri kir, yar berśel ri quf, yar quflel ri ber;
Ber berśel ri kir, ber quflel ri yar;
Quf quflel ri kir.

Gá meda, núge tógea; tagge kefgea; nhonge lengea.
