Akoryde.


Andalib, Andalib. Tell me the suchness of the loudmote, and tell me the name of its yoke and flock. Tell me of its beaming shade, and tell me of its glowing shape. Tell me where the blocksome and the edgesome grow their splitting fields.

This world is very loud, and this world has many louds. To understand the loud, is to yoke its mote into its flock. Indeed, the minding loudbit loudmesh of loudness stretches lainwise by its kind of berth and stoodwise by its kind of way. Look!

The glowing kinds of berth1 are eight—
The lippy liploud, the toothy toothloud, the cuppy cuploud, the roofy roofloud,
The thatchy thatchloud2, the leeky leekloud, the wardy wardloud3, the throaty throatloud.

The glowing kinds of way are eight—
The blasting blaster, the fizzing fizzer, the nosing noser, the tapping tapper,
The leaning leaner, the gliding glider, the pushing pusher, the clicking clicker.

The glide of the highest lift, the glide is the loud that glows without bond.

The lean of the higher lift, the lean is the loud that frontwise chokes, that bothwise glows.
The nose of the higher lift, the nose is the loud that herewise chokes, that upwise glows.

The tap of the lift4, the tap is the loud that chokes, that glows.
The fizz of the lift, the fizz is the loud that almost chokes, that barely glows.

The blast of the land, the blast is the loud that chokes in bond.
The push5 of the land, the blast is the loud that chokes in bond.
The click of the land, the blast is the loud that chokes in bond. Look!

At once the word (the maniwort (the root (THE HEART THE REAR) THE HEAD))!

The loud is the faying of berth and way, The mote is the faying of axled mark,
To know the loud is to know the tung, To know the mote is to understand,
Eightwise outright drawing one. Eightwise upright eking three.

The mote is the block, the mote is the edge,
The mote is smooth and the mote is staved,
    The mote is sheer, or nosed, or leant,
The mote lies down and the mote is seven.
The mote is the fore, the mote is the blade,
    The mote is ringed, or tipped, or raised,
The mote is the hilt and the mote is the root,
    The mote is nearfar, or liftland, or tightlax, or almonds,
The mote stands up and the mote is eleven.

The louds tell eight by eight6, but the motes tell seven and seventy7.
Then thirteen hidden and unspeaksome loudmotes linger outside this world.
Are they kind— or are they cind8?

1Glowing hues here and below, taken from writ.
2Or saily sailloud in Ff.
3Or gardy gardloud.
4Perhaps high lift or even height.
5Or TAng. draw, see also TAng. shoot.
6Or eightfold eight.
7Or seveneke seventy.
8Meaning unknown; seems to rime with kind.
