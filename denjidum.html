I. Study on Holy Sonnet XIV.

Translated from Kɛlɛmdɔnr kuḍuzɔyidɛ al kuḍuẓɔḥnibɛ.

The sun is yoked to heavens shooting star,
So stand she must before her massive fate.
When they approach, their glowing bonds create
A beam ablaze that pierces ever far.

So shall the sun avert her gaze ajar
Until her cataclysm doth abate;
Or shall some chasm bend their bond too straight
And haply quell the brilliance of her char;

Neither! look, the star is doubly-bound:
Her path towards the sun is reinèd by
Some distant seasons steady ebb and flow.

Her shining threads may never be unwound
As times advance is made to nullify.
So pass the twain, and wends their rigid glow.


II. Study on “The Tyger” and “The Lamb”.

Translated from the notes of Karɛl Hrbafɛk and Tɔmaf Yɛḥ.

Cardinal, cardinal, burned with the brand called
Cardinal, blazing, thou beckonst the bosk to
Bond and combust with thee, bond and grow brighter—
Brighter! and boundless, her brilliance imbibèd.

Showered in rainfall and quietly perching,
Ordinal, ordinal, ne’er to do mischief,
Leadeth thy kindred, the juvenile into
Helical gardens that fillen in excess.
