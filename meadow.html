Meadow where snakes wander.

And it came to pass that on that Wednesday evening, all of the reptiles were gathered at the swamp, which they called rverkhàrkháa /əχàχáː/. And lo, the mighty saltwater crocodile, who was called rgáarkhéz /ɢáːχḛ́/, proclaimed unto the fellowship of reptiles, “Reptiles! Let us give thanks. The waters of this earth travel through marsh and swamp and bog and fen. Indeed, there is no wetland that is not touched by this substance that gives us life.”

And behold, there was a great cheer throughout the congregation. And the lizard (whom they called váahqá /ʢá̤ːʜáː/, for she was the wisest of the reptiles) began to speak, saying “Let us remember rgheez /ʁə̰ː/, which was given to us by our mother khúz /xṵ́/ (who is today called Carnufex), so that her death three hundred myriad generations before us is not in vain.”

When she had finished saying this, the reptiles marvelled, for a great tapestry ascended from the waters, and upon it were inscribed three great images. But the other reptiles did not understand what the images meant, and so the lizard spoke unto them, saying:

“O ye blessed reptiles, the three images ye see before you are the three great sound-families with which we have been blessed.

“And I tell you the truth, all vowels are built upon the backs of our throats, and whereas we may think of the fundamental essences as three, the universal form is three upon three upon two upon three, and the three of three is mightier than all other threes, and all other twos.

“And behold, I speak the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I tell thee the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I may speak the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I may tell thee the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I declare, thou must speak the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I declare, thou must tell me the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I speak not the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I tell thee not the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I may speak not the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I may tell thee not the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I declare not, thou must speak the truth today, and then yesterday, and then I declare not, thou must tell me the truth today, and then yesterday, and so for the other works and deeds.

“And recall, I am kxáah and thou art kxáaz and the other is kxá, and we are rgxúh and you are rgxúz and the others are rgxúu, but when I render unto myself, I say kxàah, and so for all fellows, and when I render what is mine, I say kxàah, and so for all fellows.

“And mirad, so I say gzéazvhaa kxu rgxùz rghà rvùrvárvù. kéezqerkuz kxu rgurgù. qhávaagheh keezqèrkúz khùzvhàa. rgxáagurveh rgxùuz khùzvhàa vèezrgha kxaa.”

When she had finished saying all of this, the reptiles were amazed. And at that time, it had come to pass that all of the wights had gathered at the swamp. And mighty Nix sat upon the moss, and the migs did gather about mighty Nix, and the wigs were likewise gathered about mighty Nix.
