Preliminary notes on the grammar of rgheez.

The Crocodile language is a member of the Crocodilian branch of the Reptilian language family spoken by several dozen million crocodilians in the southern tropics. It is related to the Squamatian, Tuatarian, and Testudian languages, and more distantly to the Avian languages. Here, the language will be denoted by its endonym, “rgheez” /ʁə̰ː/ (no quotes).

The curious reader may pause to analyze for themselves the below data from rgheez, before they proceed to the description that follows.

The grammar now follows.

The rgheez language is a lightly-fusional head-initial language characterised by a rich inventory of dorsal and radical consonants.

There are fifteen (15) pulmonic consonants, four (4) non-pulmonic consonants, and three (3) segmentally-contrastive vowels in rgheez. Phonologically, the pulmonic consonants can be defined using the features $[\pm\ \text{front}]$, $[\pm\ \text{continuant}]$, $[\pm\ \text{voice}]$, and $[\pm\ \text{peripheral}]$. The non-pulmonic consonants are only attested in word-initial position, and are mostly restricted to reptilian or functional lexica.

The vowel inventory of rgheez contrasts only three vowel heights; however, vowels carry a number of suprasegmental features. There are contrasts between short and long vowels; between breathy, creaky, and modal voice; and between high, low, and neutral tone. Tone is both lexical and grammatical, and neutral tone is phonetically middle. In total, then, there are fifty-four (54) vowel contrasts. Their orthographical forms are given.

A brief optimality-theoretic analysis of rgheez verb forms is given. Most rgheez verbs have three vowels, all of which are tonally neutral until conjugated. There are three relevant alternations here. Perhaps most evidently, tone spreads rightwards to any sequences of tonally-neutral vowels. In addition, a sequence of /a/ followed by another vowel causes coalescence, and finally, radical consonants trigger vowel backing and block tonal spreading.

These constraints are summarised below.

M: draw
Vowels agree in \([\pm\ \text{front}]\) with neighbouring consonants.
M: reave
/a/ reaves a subsequent vowel.
M: spread
Each toneless vowel copies the tone of its predecessor.
F: mire
A vowel that follows a \([-\ \text{front}]\) consonant does not copy tone.
F: max
Outputs maximise maps from the input.

A medley of other subjects is now briefly discussed. First, recall quickly that rgheez syntax is verb-initial and head-initial.

Rgheez uses a base-eighty numeral system; place values alternate between base eight and base ten. For example, 9 is succeeded by 10, 79 is succeeded by 100, 979 is succeeded by 1000, 7979 is succeeded by 10000, and so on. Odd-powered digits are marked with \([-\ \text{long}]\), giving long numbers the appearance of an accordion.

“All animals are born free and equal. They are endowed with reason and conscience and should act towards one another in fellowship.”
