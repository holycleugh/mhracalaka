Flossing patterns.

On 2 July 2017, I began to use a 200-yard spool of dental floss. I used it nightly until 7 April 2018, when I came down to the final 0.5 inches. I have organised below some statistics for my viewing pleasure.

  • Days flossed: 280 days
  • Average daily string use: 25.714 inches
  • Cost of floss per day*: $0.014

Then we can conclude that I have made my use of dental floss more economical.

On 17 November 2016, I purchased a 200-metre spool of dental floss for $3.92 (before sales tax). I used it nightly until 1 July 2017, when I came down to the final 6.5 inches. I have organised below some statistics for my viewing pleasure.

  • Cost of floss per day†: $0.0173
  • Length of floss prescribed by dentist: 45.72 cm (18.00 in)
  • Days flossed, with the 6.5-inch outlier: 226
  • Average length of floss per day: 88.50 cm (34.84 in)
  • Days flossed, without the 6.5-inch outlier: 225
  • Average length of floss per day: 88.82 cm (34.97 in)

*Mirad, I have made the assumption that the cost of floss is constant across time.
†Mirad, I have assumed no sales tax and a flat cost per day independent of string length.
