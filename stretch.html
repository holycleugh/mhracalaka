The hoard $H$ is the many that is unempty and unfull.

The unmany $\O$ is the many that is empty, and so inholds nothing.

The true tell hoard $\T$ is the hoard that inholds the unmany, and the faying of some thing in the hoard with the onemany of that thing, for every thing in the hoard.

The full tell hoard $\F$ is the many that inholds every flight of cleft tells, whose things become twainwise unboundedly close.
In truth, the full tell hoard is too great to belist its things.

And so the unending fourfold tell hoard is the full tell hoard, and nothing else.

Now, the full map is the map from the full tell hoard to itself,
Called steady from some thing if every landed open neighbourhood is open,
And steady everywhere, or steady, if it is steady everywhere,
And otherwise unsteady somewhere.

The maphoard is the hoard of maps, called steady if every inheld map is steady,
And every map of the forsteady maphoard is besuched by one utleast bound on steadiness,
And one utleast bound on steadiness suchens every map of the samesteady maphoard,
And the twofold steady maphoard is bothsteady, and the onefold steady is neither.

The flight is the map from the true tell hoard to some hoard, or else that hoard itself,
And the unending flight is the inflight, and so is the inmap from the true tell hoard to some hoard,
And the meeting unending flight is the flight that becomes unboundedly close to some frame.

The flightfay is the faying of every thing in the flight,
And the unending flightfay likewise, and the meeting unending flightfay and its frame likewise.

And so behold henceforth the eight thousand maps of the full maphoard, and nothing else.

The steady full map shatters into the shard, and the meted full map gathers into the gard.

The map that shatters once is the once shattersome map,
And the map that shatters twice is the twice shattersome map, and so on.
The map that shatters unendingly is the smooth map,
And the steady map that does not shatter somewhere is the rough map,
And the steady map that does not shatter anywhere is the jagged map.

The unendingly great tell is the tell without the span between each true tell and its flip,
    And is unendingly great or nought, or otherwise its shade is unendingly great.
The eversmall is the unendingly small tell, and the evergreat aughtwise,
The ever tell hoard is the faying of the full tell hoard, with every eversmall and every evergreat.
The unendingly close twain of tells is unendingly small in unlikeness, and so is everclose.

The shardmap is the map of the shard of some steady map,
Given by the eversmall span lifted, and then cleft by it, for every thing everywhere,
And the eversmall things forgotten.
