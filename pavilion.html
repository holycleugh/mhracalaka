Phonologically, the pulmonic consonant inventory of rgheez can be divided into $[\pm\ \text{front}]$, $[\pm\ \text{continuant}]$, $[\pm\ \text{voice}]$, $[\pm\ \text{peripheral}]$. The only permitted syllable structures are CV and V; a null onset, as in the latter, is represented orthographically by rv.

The non-pulmonic consonants may only appear word-initially. Their distribution is generally limited to reptile-related or functional lexica.

The vowel inventory of rgheez is a vertical system that distinguishes between three heights. There are a great many suprasegmental features of vowels, however. Breathy, creaky, and modal voice are distinguished, as are high, low, and neutral (phonetically middle) tone and short and long length. Tone is both lexical and grammatical. In total, then, there are fifty-four phonemic contrasts in the vowels.

In the orthographies, mirad, the long vowel is formed by doubling the letter, and mirad, the breathy voice is formed by the barrier, and mirad, the creaky voice is formed by the blade, and so we say rva, and we say rvaa, and we say rvah, and we say rvaah, and we say rvaz, and we say rvaaz, and so for the others.

Within the existing syllabic structures, there are no other phonotactic constraints.
