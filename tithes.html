Spring wends into summer,
 summer wends into harvest,
  harvest wends into winter,
   and winter wends into spring;
    and frolick, frolick, frolick, frolick;
    and frolick, frolick, frolick, frolick;
    and frolick, frolick, frolick, frolick;
    and frolick, frolick, frolick, frolick.

To-day is harvest day. Look out the window, for Lent-passers are passing by—

See, has yet the ethel to come, and so can I not naked into the cold out-step.

Hew down the chuckling tree, and cut off its branches;
  shake off its leaves and scatter its fruit.

Sevenfold of this, and may the chuckling tree be as the enemies of David are.
